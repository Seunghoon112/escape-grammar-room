Grammar Mission

Escape the Grammar Room

Read the clues carefully. The doors will open as long as your team understands the condition. If your answer is approved, your teacher will enter the open key.

▶ Welcome to the Grammar Room

You are locked in the Grammar Room. To escape, you must open five locks. Work with your team, write your answers, and call your teacher when you are ready.

Today’s key expression is as long as.
It can mean “~하는 한 / ~하기만 하면” when it shows a condition.
You will also meet if and unless in the missions.

1 Lock 1. The Key of Bad Luck

The first door opens as long as you choose the key filled with bad luck.
The biggest key is too proud.
The brightest key is trying to trick you.
Choose the key that carries the number people fear.

Key A: a big golden key with the number 12
Key B: a small silver key with the number 13
Key C: a shiny red key with the number 7
Teacher Check
If your answer is correct, your teacher will enter the open key. Unless the open key is entered, the next door will stay closed.

2 Lock 2. Teacher Battle

You can pass through the second door as long as you beat the teacher in Rock-Paper-Scissors.
If you lose, complete the Retry Mission.
Unless you complete the mission or beat the teacher, the door will stay closed.
Offline Mission
Choose one team representative and play Rock-Paper-Scissors with the teacher.
If your team wins, your teacher can open this lock.
If your team loses, complete the Retry Mission first.
Retry Mission
Solve one textbook example on your worksheet.
You can try again as long as you solve one textbook example.
Teacher Check
If the team wins the battle or completes the Retry Mission, enter the open key. Unless the open key is entered, the next door will stay closed.

3 Lock 3. The Time Password

Find the sentence that gives you time.
If you find the correct sentence, explain why it gives you time.
Unless your teacher approves your explanation, the password will not work.

A. You can escape as long as you find the final key.
Password: ESCAPE

B. Take as long as you need, but do not look back.
Password: NEED

C. You are safe as long as you stay together.
Password: SAFE
Teacher Check
Say your answer to the teacher. If your explanation is accepted, your teacher will enter the open key. Unless the open key is entered, the next door will stay closed.

4 Lock 4. The Reversed Spell

If your team reads the sentence correctly, the spell will disappear.
The spell will disappear as long as your team reads the sentence correctly.

Now change it into an unless sentence with the same meaning.
If your sentence keeps the same meaning, the spell can disappear.
Unless your sentence is checked by the teacher, the spell will stay.
Teacher Check
Read your sentence aloud to the teacher. If the sentence is correct, your teacher will enter the open key. Unless the open key is entered, the next door will stay closed.

5 Lock 5. The Final English Class Rule

The final door will open as long as your class makes one rule for this room.
Make one English class rule or classroom rule using as long as.
If your rule is meaningful, the final door can open.
Unless your teacher approves the rule, the Grammar Room will not let you out.

Possible Sentence Frames

We can have a great English class as long as we ____________________.
We can have a better classroom as long as we ____________________.
We can enjoy school life as long as we ____________________.
listen to each other try our best respect each other don’t fall asleep speak in English help our friends keep our classroom clean
Teacher Check
Share your rule with the teacher. If your rule uses as long as properly, your teacher will enter the final open key. Unless the final open key is entered, you cannot escape.

✓ You Escaped!

Congratulations!

You escaped the Grammar Room as long as your team understood the clues, completed the missions, and received the teacher’s open keys.

Remember:
where explains a place.
as long as can show a condition or time/duration.
unless means “if not.”
