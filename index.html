Grammar Mission

Escape the Grammar Room

Read the clues carefully. The doors will open as long as you understand the condition.

▶ Welcome to the Grammar Room

You are locked in the Grammar Room. To escape, you must open five locks. Work with your team and write important answers on your worksheet.

Today’s key expression is as long as.
It can mean “~하는 한 / ~하기만 하면” when it shows a condition.

1 Lock 1. The Key of Bad Luck

The first door opens as long as you choose the key filled with bad luck.
The biggest key is too proud.
The brightest key is trying to trick you.
Choose the key that carries the number people fear.

2 Lock 2. Teacher Battle

You can pass through the second door as long as you beat the teacher in Rock-Paper-Scissors.
If you lose, complete the Retry Mission.
Unless you get the teacher’s secret key, the door will stay closed.
Offline Mission
1. Choose one team representative.
2. Play Rock-Paper-Scissors with the teacher.
3. If you win, ask the teacher to enter the secret key.
4. If you lose, complete the Retry Mission first.

Retry Mission

Write this textbook sentence in a full sentence on your worksheet:
As long as you are happy and healthy, nothing else matters.

교사용 해제키 기본값: OPEN2 — 수업 전에 코드에서 바꿀 수 있음.

3 Lock 3. The Time Password

Find the sentence that gives you time.
The password is under that sentence.

4 Lock 4. The Reversed Spell

If your team reads the sentence correctly, the spell will disappear.
The spell will disappear as long as your team reads the sentence correctly.

Now change it into an unless sentence with the same meaning.

Choose the correct sentence.

정답을 고른 뒤, 모둠이 함께 문장을 소리 내어 읽으면 주문이 해제됩니다.

5 Lock 5. The Final English Class Rule

The final door will open as long as your class makes one rule for this room.
Make one English class rule using as long as.

Sentence Frame

We can have a great English class as long as we ____________________.
listen to each other try our best respect each other don’t fall asleep speak in English help our friends

✓ You Escaped!

Congratulations!

You escaped the Grammar Room because you understood the conditions.

Remember:
where explains a place.
as long as can show a condition or time/duration.
